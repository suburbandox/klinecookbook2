Thai Hot and Sour Soup (Dom Yam Gung)<br><br>
1 T vegetable oil<br> 4 citrus leaves<br> shells from shrimp<br> 1 t lime zest, slivered<br> 8 cups chicken stock<br> 2 lbs. shrimp (20/lb.) shelled and deveined<br> 1 1/2 t salt<br> 3 stalks lemongrass cut into 1" length<br> 2 green serrano chilies<br> 1 T fish sauce (Nam Pla)<br> 1 red Serrano chili<br> juice of 2 limes<br> 2 T cilantro, chopped coarsely<br> 3 green onions (including some green) coarsely chopped.<br><br>

Heat oil in saucepan and fry shells until they turn pink. <br>Add chicken stock, salt, lemongrass, citrus leaves, lime rind and green chilies. Bring to boil, cover, reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes. <br>Strain mixture through a sieve, return the liquid to a saucepan and bring to boil. Add the shrimp to this boiling stock and cook them for 2-3 minutes. <br>Reduce heat to simmer and add the fish sauce and lime juice. <br>Stir and immediately remove from heat to prevent overcooking. Pour soup in tureen, sprinkle with red chilies, coriander leaves and green onion. Serve piping hot.
